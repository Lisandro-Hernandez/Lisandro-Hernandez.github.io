# 📊 van der Waals Equation of State

## 🧠 Overview
A great deal of infomration can be extracted from equations of state. Here is shown the isotherms, liquid-vapor phase diagram, liquid-vapor densities and heats of vaporization as a function of temperature.

## 🔍 Description
- Critical and subcritical isotherms of nitrongen as predicted by the van der Waals equation of state.
- Liquid-vapor line of the nitrogen phase diagram as predicted by the van der Waals equation of state. Point A represents the van
der Waals critical point (which is ultimately determined by the values of a and b). Point B represents the experimental critical point of this gas.
- Densities of the coexisting liquid and vapor nitrogen phases as a function of temperature.
- Heat of vaporization comparison between predicted via van der Waals and experimental for argon.

## 💡 Key Insights
- The liquid-vapor phase transition region decreases as the system approaches the critical temperature. At the critical temperature, the liquid-vapor phase transition region becomes a point.
- Due to the disagreement in the critical points predicted and experimental (points A and B in the phase diagram) the van der Waals liquid-vapor
equilibrium line is expected to disagree with the experimental one.
- As the critical point is approached along the liquid-gas coexistence line in the phase diagram, the difference between the liquid and the vapor phase becomes increasingly less distinct until the difference disappears entirely at the critical point. This is why the liquid and vapor densities approach each other and become equal at the critical point. The liquid phase and vapor phase simply merge into a single fluid phase. 
- Heat of vaporization is alsways positive as this is an endothermic process. As the system approaches the critical point both phases merge into a single phase and the enthalpy change associated to the transformation vanishes
